The Vision & Objectives

The Micro- and Nanoelectronics specialty aims to train expert engineers capable of designing, fabricating, modeling, and characterizing miniaturized electronic systems.

We address the rapid evolution of Integrated Circuits, Flexible Electronics, Smart Sensors, and Embedded Systems to meet the growing technological demands in energy, healthcare, IoT, and cybersecurity.

> Aligning with national tech sovereignty and global R&D standards.

Curriculum Roadmap

S1-S2

Foundational Physics & Electronics

Semiconductor Physics, Analog/Digital Electronics, Thin Films, Control Systems.

Goal: Master the atomic logic and electron transport phenomena.

S3-S4

Advanced Design & Modeling

VLSI Circuit Design, Electronic Design Automation (EDA), Microcontrollers, Microtech.

Goal: Transition from transistor-level to complex system-on-chip architectures.

S5

Specialization & Smart Systems

Piezoelectric Micro-Nanodevices, Smart Sensor Networks, Multiphysics Modeling.

Goal: Interface the physical world with the digital mesh via NEMS/MEMS.

S6

Industrial Immersion (PFE)

Capstone Project / Industrial Internship.

Goal: Real-world prototyping, industry integration, and R&D capstone.
